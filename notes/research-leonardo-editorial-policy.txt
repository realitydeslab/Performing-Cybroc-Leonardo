## Editorial Scope and Focus

**Leonardo** is a **peer-reviewed international journal** published by MIT Press that focuses on work intersecting arts, sciences, and technology.[4][6] The journal specifically seeks illustrated articles by artists discussing their work alongside contributions from historians, theoreticians, philosophers, and researchers.[3][4] Leonardo addresses visual arts, music, sound art, video, performance, language, environmental, and conceptual arts—particularly those utilizing contemporary science and technology tools and materials.[3][4]

Beginning in 2021, Leonardo expanded to include music and sound content previously published separately in *Leonardo Music Journal*.[3][4]

## Submission and Formatting Requirements

All submissions must follow the **Leonardo Manuscript Template** using the **Chicago Manual of Style, 18th edition, note style** (not author-date).[1] Key formatting requirements include:

- **Abstract:** Maximum 120 words, third-person, present tense
- **Keywords:** 5–7 terms for indexing
- **References:** Numbered citations in brackets (e.g., [1], [2], [1–3]), compiled at manuscript end; avoid auto-numbering, footnotes, or superscripts[1]
- **Section Headings:** Clear, unnumbered headings with indicated levels (e.g., <1>, <2>, <3>)
- **Minimal Formatting:** Over-designing complicates production[1]
- **Word Count Limits:** Articles exceeding limits by more than 5% are rejected prior to peer review[1]

## Manuscript Components

Required elements include article type, title, author list, author information (bio, affiliation, ORCID, contact), abstract, keywords, body text, acknowledgments, references, images with captions and alt text, and biographical information (2–3 sentences).[1]

## Submission Process

Leonardo accepts both solicited and unsolicited submissions.[1] Authors are **encouraged to submit preliminary outlines** for editorial feedback before drafting complete manuscripts.[1] The publication follows a **nine-step peer review process**: initial assessment by editors, peer review by typically 2–3 field experts (3–4 months), revision rounds, and acceptance.[3] Accepted articles appear on the *Just Accepted* page within 4–6 months, with watermarked DOIs, though print publication typically requires **over three years** due to the dynamic field.[3]

**Limitation:** An author may have **no more than two articles under consideration** at any time.[5]
